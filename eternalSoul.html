Prologue

Mammon Kingdom, 1333 AD

Local peasant, Amrita Magnadyne, finds herself on death row for thievery, sentenced to execution by guillotine. One by one, the prisoners are condemned by the priest and slaughtered. Eventually, Rita is placed in the guillotine. However, just as the blade descends, a blinding light erupts from the cathedral's stained glass.

Moments later, Rita awakens to find herself alive and outside the guillotine. A hellscape lies around her. All of the townsfolk mutilated. With the castle's entrance locked behind her and many questions to be answered, she ventures inside.






Rorschach's Castle

Explore every hallway, tunnel, and crevice

A large castle awaits you. Find your way through to reach Father Rorschach, though the journey won't be so easy. Many rooms stand between you and him. Which contain demons, secret passages, and Rorschach's followers. If you fail, you can always try again. Amaranth Death will revive you at the last save point you visited.

Grow stronger as you progress by leveling up, obtaining better equipment, finding better weapons, and taking more powerful souls. Interact with various characters to learn more about everything that is happening. And take on their errands to receive rare items or money as payment.

Amaranth Death

The soul of a demon rests within you

Eventually, Rita learns that the blinding light was the soul of a demon. One named «Amaranth Death», which grants her two crucial abilities.


IMMORTALITY : No longer are you bound by mortal life. If you fall in combat, «Amaranth Death» will revive you at your previous resting point. However, other characters will remember your follies.



SOUL STEAL : Kill demons for a chance to obtain their soul and harness their power. Or steal the souls of demons within others, and use their abilities against them!





Demons and Souls

A litany of beasts roam within the castle

Many demons reside within the castle, persisting without hosts under Rorschach's influence. However, the souls they provide can be simplified into four main types.



MANIFEST : Manifest souls create some type of physical manifestation. These generally form projectile attacks such as fire balls, knives, bullets, etc. These are created instantaneously by pressing a button. Additionally, the only limit to how many can be made is how much energy you have left.



AUGMENT : Augment souls provide some type of alteration to the player. Such as a stat boost, transformation, or manipulation. Depending on the augment, a button must be held to maintain it or pressed to toggle it.



GUARDIAN : Guardian souls summon the demon itself to fight on your behalf. They can be set as completely autonomous (SENTIENT) or only acting when commanded (COMMAND). These are the most powerful and fierce but the most demanding on your energy supply.



PASSIVE : Passive souls provide a constant effect at no energy cost. These range from stat boosts to permanent abilities. These souls can be enabled/disabled inside the player's inventory. Ability-based passive souls are accessed with unique button combos. Ex: hold down and press attack while in the air.

    • Sickle Attacks

  • Sickles provide fast, weak attacks that can be jump canceled. You can effectively attack more quickly by repeating jump attacking and landing. With such a short reach, you'll be required to get closer to enemies.

Interactive Inventory

See what it does before you equip it

Have you ever gotten a weapon with mediocre stats but a powerful secret? A weapon most players would shrug off without trying because, "Eh, it does less damage than my current one." If this were Symphony of the Night, that would be the Crissaegrim or Valmanway. Well, no more! Immediately view what a weapon does from your inventory without needing to equip it.

This applies to the souls of demons as well. Open your inventory and watch the soul's ability play out without having to equip it or, more importantly, waste any energy on using it. View the soul's precise stats and capabilities. Does the manifest pierce enemies? Does the augment lock me in one place? How much damage does the guardian do in a second?
