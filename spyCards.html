Scripted Spy Cards

Play Spy Cards from Bug Fables!

Faithfully Recreated

Scripted Spy Cards is a recreation of the minigame from Bug Fables, made playable inside Tabletop Simulator. Create a deck of 15 cards (1 boss card, 2 mini-boss cards, and 12 enemy cards). Mix and match cards or focus on a single tribe. There are many ways to create your deck and win. Once you've built your deck, you can save it and begin battling your friends online.





Battle Your Friends

Or spectate

Once you create a room in Tabletop Simulator, you can invite up to eight friends. Two battling and six spectating. The two battling one another can't peek at the cards in the other's hand. They'll only show their face once placed down. However, spectators can see both players' cards at all times. Whether they're still being held or have been placed.


Once a competing player is ready, they press their corresponding button to ready up. Once both players are ready, the cards will lock, and the round begins.

Let The Round Begin

Sit back and watch

Once the round begins, the game will automatically start moving the cards, calculating attack and defense, applying effects, and more. Once the round is over, the loser will lose one health point. Cards are then collected, held, and redistributed throughout the match. So think twice before placing that valuable boss card. The game's "Auto-Dealer" will be holding onto it for a while before you get it back.

Final Thoughts

Pseudo dev-log

This project only happened because my friend thought it would be fun to play the Spy Cards minigame from Bug Fables online with friends. In Bug Fables, the card game would only be played with a few NPCs, hence being a minigame. I figured Tabletop Simulator would be a great fit, so I set out to learn how to make mods in it. And how to program in Lua.


There were many strange roadblocks I had to overcome. For example, some cards will summon (created from thin air) other cards to be played in the same round. This wasn't easy to incorporate, but I eventually got it to work. Annoyingly I never found a way to actually summon a card from nothing. Tabletop Simulator was very restrictive with what your Lua code could do. The one solution I found was to have locked template cards copied when a card had to be summoned.


Overall, I enjoyed working on this and finally playing Spy Cards online with my friend. It turned out well; logically, it was a one-to-one match with Bug Fables. Both versions played identically. This is especially great since I didn't have access to the source code and had to figure out the logic through trial and error. This card game may seem simple, but it can get very complicated.
