A Strange Temple

Will you journey inside?

Deep within Central America lies a long-forgotten Aztec temple. Thought to be a myth, it has now mysteriously been found. Ancient stories and rumors of this temple have been passed down for generations. All speaking of a legendary gem with an ability like no other. What mystical powers does this crystal hold?


You've been sent by a research team to investigate the temple and obtain the crystal. However, upon entering, you become trapped inside! The only way out is through the temple.





The Crystal Within

Can you master it?

With the mystical power of the crystal, you can now stop time! Along with it is a small pocket watch, but it doesn't seem to keep track of time. Instead, it keeps track of the time scale! As well as tracking how much longer time may remain stopped. With these two items in hand, you must journey forward and persevere through.


Is this your key to escape? What are the limits of this new ability? Can you master using it in order to solve the puzzles?

Dominance over time!

An ancient power awoken

The crystal allows you to stop time for 12 seconds. During which all objects around you will halt in place, and all saturation is drained around you. Using these few seconds wisely, you must solve parts of the puzzle at a time. Or rush through and learn new tricks. Once the 12 seconds are up, time will resume. After which, the crystal will slowly recharge and grant another 12 seconds.


You'll learn how physics changes while time is stopped and how to use these changes to your advantage.





Trials and Tribulations

Solve the temporal puzzles

Several rooms lie between you and the exit, each with a unique puzzle. Grab blocks and stop time to create bridges suspended above pits of lava. Stop doors from closing after running from a pressure plate. And utilize gears that behave differently depending on whether time is or isn't stopped.


Complete the puzzles and escape from the temple. Remember your goal and the research team that sent you. They eagerly await your return.

Final Thoughts

Pseudo dev-log

Starting on this project was nerve-wracking at first. I hardly had any prior Unity experience, yet I willingly signed up for the Advanced Game Design class knowing full well I would be required to create something in it. Nonetheless, I'm glad I did it. I learned a lot through this development experience. It kickstarted my love of game development and ultimately led to me starting work on Eternal Soul a few months later.


The entirety of this project went through tight deadlines. I was already juggling two jobs, other senior projects, and the rest of my final classes. So I'm partially surprised with how it turned out. It isn't perfect, and if given a chance, I'd go back and do some things differently (I was never happy with how the lighting turned out). But for a short college project, I think it exceeded the class expectations.
